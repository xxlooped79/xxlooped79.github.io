The Moment I Realized My Professor Is a Person Too

Coming into college, I thought professors were basically academic robots. They showed up, lectured for an hour, assigned homework, and disappeared into their offices until the next class. I never really questioned that idea until one small moment completely changed how I see my professors.


A Small Comment That Changed Everything

One day after class, my professor casually mentioned how they still get nervous before presenting at conferences. That surprised me. This was someone who confidently lectures in front of over a hundred students, yet they admitted to feeling the same anxiety many of us feel before presentations.

In that moment, I realized professors are not just authority figures. They are people who once sat in the same desks we do now, worrying about grades, deadlines, and whether they were good enough.

It made the classroom feel less intimidating and more human.

Why That Matters as a Student

Seeing professors as real people made me more comfortable asking questions and participating in class. Instead of worrying about sounding stupid, I remembered that learning is a process, even for experts.

College is not just about memorizing information. It is about connecting with ideas and the people who teach them. When professors show their humanity, it reminds students that growth does not stop after graduation.

This experience changed how I approach class in a few important ways:

  • I feel more comfortable asking questions
  • I participate more during lectures
  • I see learning as an ongoing process

Now, when I walk into class, I do not just see someone grading my assignments. I see a person who chose to teach, to learn, and to keep improving, just like us.
